# Academic plot style

- Use a white background, high contrast, sans-serif typography, and light dashed or
  dotted grids behind the data.
- Use colorblind-safe categorical palettes and perceptually uniform sequential maps
  such as Viridis, Magma, or Plasma. Use a restrained diverging map for signed values;
  never use Jet/Rainbow.
- Distinguish groups with markers, line styles, or hatches as well as color.
- Use solid lines for primary measurements and dashed lines for baselines or theory.
  Add markers at observed data points and shaded bands for interval uncertainty.
- Keep category labels horizontal unless overlap requires rotation. Prefer direct
  labels or compact legends placed without covering data.
- Annotate heatmap cells with exact values when readable. Use square cells and thin or
  absent separators.
- Use clear bar grouping and consistently styled capped error bars. Avoid decorative
  3D effects and misleading truncated axes.
- Make titles, labels, units, scales, tick formatting, and precision consistent with
  the source data.
